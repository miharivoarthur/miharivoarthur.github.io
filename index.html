About Me

I am a doctoral student in the Structural Mechanics and Dynamics Laboratory at Saitama University. My research focuses on assessing the effectiveness of silicone rubber in seismic isolation bearings through experimental studies. I specialize in continuum mechanics with a growing focus on hyperelastic materials like rubber.

Research

My research explores advanced techniques in seismic isolation to improve the safety and resilience of structures. I use silicone rubber in seismic isolation bearings to mitigate the effects of earthquake forces.

Projects

  • Seismic Isolation Bearings: Developed experimental models for silicone rubber bearings.
  • Continuum Mechanics Simulations: Applied continuum mechanics principles to simulate stress and deformation in hyperelastic materials.
  • MATLAB and Python Simulations: Created computational models for structural dynamic analysis and simulation.
